Activities

Fun Fly

The West Potomac Fun Fly is a day of fun for the whole family! People of all ages can come together and connect over aviation and flight. Anyone in the community can fly their indoor powered RC planes or their lightweight rubber band powered “free flight” planes. Kids can play on a RC plane flight simulator on the computer, or they can create their own paper plate planes at the kid’s table. Mr. Franco's Aerospace 1 students fly their own designed planes for a grade in their class.

Young children have a lot of fun at the fun fly! Here a child is guided by two TSA members while using the flight simulator

The powered flight section is full of avid indoor flyers and their RC planes.

Many members of the West Potomac TSA chapter volunteer at this event, helping with setup and cleanup, manning the reception desk, selling concessions, and making announcements.

NVRC Auction

Northern Virginia Radio Control (NVRC) is one of the largest radio control clubs in Virginia with over 200 members. The club includes pilots in all areas of radio-controlled flight: Sport, Giant Scale, Electric, Sailplane, Aerobatics, Combat, and MultiRotor. NVRC has members with decades of expertise to share in many types of flying and building.

West Potomac TSA hosts NVRC’s biannual auction. At the auction, thousands of dollars worth of RC planes and parts are traded, and the TSA chapter gets a percentage of the profits for being the host. At the event, members of TSA help setup, cleanup, and sell concessions.

RC Mania

A brochure advertising RC Mania and listing out concessions prices and sponsors.

RC Mania was once the largest fundraising event of the year for TSA. It was a weekend event in which families could come and participate in many fun activities. For hobbyists, there were spaces for flying RC planes, captaining RC boats, and driving RC cars. For the young ones, there were pony rides and a face painting booth. This was such as large event that all of the outdoor sports playing fields were needed to fit all of these activities.

Unfortunately, with the tightening of FAA rules on recreational flight, the biggest draw for the event was lost, and the event was cancelled. The last RC Mania occurred in 2015.

Boats and cars and planes, oh my!

Engineers Week

The most important week of the year on the West Potomac TSA calendar is National Engineers Week, or E-week. It is a time for us to celebrate the difference that engineers make in the world, increase public dialogue about the need for engineers, and bring the field to life for kids, educators, and parents everywhere.

These 7 days in February are a great opportunity for the chapter to raise awareness for the chapter around the school. The whole week is scheduled with school-wide spirit days, where people are encouraged to wear outfits based on various engineering-related themes.

To celebrate, we paint the giant school rock in front of the building with the TSA logo and engineering-related designs. This is a scale model of the rock painted in the similarly to the actual rock in front of the school.

An underrepresented demographic in the engineering field is women. During E-week, TSA invites female students to visit the CTE classrooms by putting flyers around the school, and hosting an event where female students who are already in the program speak to a group of visitors, encouraging them to join.

Arcade

For years, generations of TSA members worked on a full scale retro arcade cabinet, finally completing it in 2015. Inside, there is a Windows 7 PC running an emulator that allows players to play 40 classic arcade games, with just a quarter.

The arcade machine, complete with a custom West Potomac themed paint job.

Students have a lot of fun playing on the arcade (when they've finished their work, of course).

This year, TSA will host the first ever WPHS Video Game Tournament, where students of the school are invited to come and compete playing the Japanese fighting game Tekken.

YDA Partnership

The Youth Development Academy (YDA), formerly Governor’s (GYDA), is an organization that provides opportunities for students of underrepresented groups or underprivileged families to experience many different fields. The West Potomac TSA is a proud partner of the YDA. Annually, we do a presentation and activities to encourage members to join engineering.

One of the activities was to challenge YDA members to create the tallest paper structure to withstand the most wind.

Members of the YDA having fun at the TSA presentation.

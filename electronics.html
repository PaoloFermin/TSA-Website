Electronics 1

Course includes material on electrical and electronic theory and applications, circuit simulation, circuit troubleshooting, components, use of test equipment, consumer information and career opportunities. This is accomplished through classroom lecture, experimentation, demonstration, and practical application with projects. Students will be prepared to either enter the electronics career field or continue their education in Electronics Engineering. Students interested in engineering or related technical careers will benefit from this course.



An electronics student uses an analog oscilloscope to test a circuit.

The final project of an electronics 1 student. This is a “Piano Lock Box”, with a compartment in the back that remains locked until the proper melody is played on the keyboard in the front. It contains an Arduino Mega 2560. The wooden casing and keys were laser cut using the Zing laser cutter in the Prototyping room.

Electronics students take an in depth hands on approach to learning about electronics components, such as creating their own DC motors from a kit.

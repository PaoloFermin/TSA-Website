The Real World vs. Augmented Reality vs. Virtual Reality

When would you want to use augmented reality? Augmented Reality may sound similar to it’s cousin, virtual reality, but they are two distinctly different systems. Where augmented reality merely adds to the real world, virtual reality completely replaces it. Essentially, augmented reality bridges the gap between the real and virtual worlds. Source: Milgram, Takemura, Utsumi, & Kishino, 1994

As with anything, there are tradeoffs to using technologies along this continuum. As you move right along the line, the technologies listed become more and more separate from the real world, until finally reaching virtual reality, where the real world is completely replaced by computer generated imagery. From the perspective of a user, the virtual environment allows for the most freedom and customizability, while at the cost of more resources. At the moment, virtual reality headsets must be attached to a desktop PC in order to run. On the other hand, the real environment has no customizability, but obviously, people can experience it without needing any hardware at all. Augmented reality is in the middle of these two extremes. AR can run on small devices such as a phone for small applications, such as displaying a simple image, or it can use immersive HMDs such as the Microsoft Hololens.

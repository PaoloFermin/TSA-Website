STEM Advanced Engineering and Emerging Technologies

This advanced level engineering course emphasizes student knowledge and application of engineering problem-solving through research and development concepts. It focuses on current and emerging technologies. Students learn to work in collaborative engineering teams and solve group design and projects. Students are challenged to solve problems using design, mathematics, computer simulations, scientific analysis, and oral and written skills. Engineering is studied with a focus on modeling, systems, optimization, technology-society interaction, design, and engineering ethics. Projects may be models, systems, or products that creatively solve an engineering problem. This course follows the STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) directive. This course is the capstone course in the engineering sequence to prepare students for college or engineering careers.



Students created their own school-friendly version of the popular competition known as Battlebots, where robots attempt to destroy each other in an arena.

At the end of the year, students go on a field trip to a nearby lake, where they race cardboard boats of their own design.
